How to Play Chess

A complete beginner's guide — from setting up the board to making your first moves and winning your first game.

Step 1: Setting Up the Board

Place the chess board so each player has a light-coloured square in the bottom-right corner. The board has 64 squares (8×8).

  1. Place Rooks in the corner squares of the first row.
  2. Place Knights next to the Rooks.
  3. Place Bishops next to the Knights.
  4. Place the Queen on the square matching her colour (white Queen on light square).
  5. Place the King on the last remaining square.
  6. Fill the entire second row with 8 Pawns.
💡 Remember: "Queen on her colour" — white Queen starts on a light square, black Queen on a dark square.

Step 3: How Turns Work

White moves first. Players alternate turns, one piece per turn. You cannot skip your turn.

  1. Click a piece — valid moves are highlighted.
  2. Click a highlighted square to move there.
  3. Landing on an enemy piece captures it.
  4. Your turn ends; your opponent moves next.

Step 4: Check and Checkmate

When your King is attacked it is in check — you must escape it immediately by: moving the King, blocking the attack, or capturing the attacker. If none work, it is checkmate and the game ends.

⚠️ You can never make a move that leaves your own King in check.

Step 5: Special Moves

Castling

King moves 2 squares toward a Rook; the Rook jumps to the other side. Only when neither has moved, no pieces between them, and King not in check or crossing attacked squares.

En Passant

If an enemy pawn moves 2 squares beside your pawn, you may capture it as if it moved only 1 square — but only on the very next move.

Pawn Promotion

When your pawn reaches the opposite end of the board, promote it to any piece. Almost always choose the Queen.

Step 6: Strategy Tips

🏰

Control the Centre

Place pieces on or near e4, d4, e5, d5 for maximum influence over the board.

⚡

Develop Early

Move Knights and Bishops out quickly in the opening so your pieces are active.

🛡️

Castle Early

Castle within the first 10 moves to put your King behind a wall of pawns.

🎯

Think Ahead

Before every move ask: can my opponent capture something for free after this?
