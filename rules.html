Chess Rules

Official rules — piece movement, special moves, winning conditions, draws, and popular openings.

Objective

Checkmate your opponent's King — put it under attack with no legal escape. If a player has no legal moves but is not in check, the result is stalemate (a draw).

Piece Movements

♔ King

Exactly one square in any direction. Cannot move to an attacked square. Can castle (see below).

♕ Queen

Any number of squares in any direction. Most powerful piece. Cannot jump over other pieces.

♖ Rook

Any number of squares horizontally or vertically. Cannot jump. Powerful on open files.

♗ Bishop

Any number of squares diagonally. Always stays on the same colour. Cannot jump.

♘ Knight

L-shape: 2 squares + 1 perpendicular. The only piece that can jump over others. Always lands on opposite colour.

♙ Pawn

Moves forward one square; two on its first move. Captures diagonally forward one square. Cannot move backward.

Special Moves

Castling

King moves 2 squares toward a Rook; Rook jumps to the other side. Not allowed if: either piece has moved, pieces between them exist, King is in check, or King passes through/lands on an attacked square.

En Passant

If a pawn advances 2 squares beside an enemy pawn, the enemy may capture it as if it moved 1 square. Must be done immediately on the very next move.

Pawn Promotion

When a pawn reaches the opposite end (rank 8 for White, rank 1 for Black), it must promote to Queen, Rook, Bishop, or Knight. Multiple Queens are allowed.

Check, Checkmate, Stalemate

Check: King is under attack. Must be resolved immediately — move King, block, or capture the attacker.

Checkmate: King in check with no legal escape. Game ends; opponent wins.

Stalemate: No legal moves, King not in check. Game ends as a draw.

Draw Conditions

  • Stalemate
  • Threefold Repetition — same position appears 3 times
  • Fifty-Move Rule — 50 moves without pawn move or capture
  • Insufficient Material — neither side can force checkmate
  • Mutual Agreement

Popular Openings

Italian Game

1.e4 e5 2.Nf3 Nc6 3.Bc4 — classic open game, rapid development.

Sicilian Defence

1.e4 c5 — Black's most popular reply; asymmetric, dynamic play.

Queen's Gambit

1.d4 d5 2.c4 — White offers a pawn for central control.

Ruy López

1.e4 e5 2.Nf3 Nc6 3.Bb5 — one of the oldest and most studied openings.

French Defence

1.e4 e6 — solid, strategic, leads to closed positions.

King's Indian

1.d4 Nf6 2.c4 g6 — Black cedes the centre then counter-attacks.

Key Tactics

  • Fork: One piece attacks two enemy pieces simultaneously.
  • Pin: An attacked piece cannot move without exposing a more valuable one.
  • Skewer: Valuable piece is in front; must move, exposing piece behind.
  • Discovered Attack: Moving one piece reveals an attack by another.
  • Zwischenzug: An intermediate move that changes the evaluation of a sequence.
